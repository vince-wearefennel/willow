WHO WE ARE

 

Working Hands was founded in 1994 by the Young Upbeat Professionals for Peace (YUPPEACE) before being spun-off as an individual organization in 1996. Since then, we've been raising and investing funds to provide out-of-school youths the opportunity to build their futures.

 

 

OUR MISSION

 

We believe in the capacity of the Filipino, the dignity of their labor, and the value of self-development. We aim to produce a new breed of technical experts equipped, not only with the necessary skills and knowledge for gainful employment, but also with strong Christian values.

 

The scholarship program we offer covers a one-year technical-vocation scholarship at any Don Bosco Training Center in the Philippines.

OUR SCHOLARS

Over 80% of our scholar graduates are known to be employed. With scholars from all around the Philippines, Working Hands has been able to offer out-of-school youth from Cebu to Nueva Ecija the opportunity to build a better future.

SAbi ko sa aking sarili, ito na ang umpisa ng aking magandang buhay..."    EDILBERTO TAYAG

"

Edilberto Tayag grew up in Pampanga, the youngest of 10. With his parents unable to afford a college education for him, he made his way to Manila, working as a janitor at Robinsons Mall, then as a messenger at a local bank.

 

Edilberto was one of our first scholars and we eagerly watched as his hard work carried him through the Automotive Mechanic course at Don  Bosco. We watched his career flourish as he moved from scholar to intern and eventually to a full-time job in Australia.

 

All we did was give him the opportunity. He built the rest with his own hands. You can help us do the same for more Filipinos around the country.

Jonathan Menguillo, our first scholar, works as an instructor at the Don Bosco Youth Center in Tondo.

Henry Britanico graduated from Don Bosco Nueva Ecija and now works as a Machinist at Asian Transmission Corporation.
